# 📊 Global Layoffs Analysis (2020–2025)

## 🧾 Project Overview
This project analyzes global layoffs data from 2020 to 2025 to understand workforce reduction patterns across industries, companies, and countries. 

The goal of this project is to extract meaningful insights using data analytics techniques and present them through visualizations and an interactive dashboard.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Identify industries with the highest layoffs
- Analyze layoffs distribution across countries
- Find companies with the largest layoffs
- Study layoff trends over time
- Understand the relationship between funding and layoffs


---

## 📂 Dataset Information
- Source: Kaggle (based on Layoffs.fyi data)
- Format: CSV
- Key Columns:
  - company
  - industry
  - country
  - total_laid_off
  - percentage_laid_off
  - funds_raised
  - stage
  - date
  - source
  - date_added

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning & Preprocessing
The dataset was cleaned and prepared using Python:
- Removed missing values in critical columns
- Removed duplicate records
- Converted date column into datetime format
- Extracted year for trend analysis
- Ensured correct data types for analysis

---

## 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

### 🔹 Univariate Analysis
- Distribution of layoffs using histogram
- Industry distribution using countplot
- Outlier detection using boxplot

### 🔹 Bivariate Analysis
- Layoffs by industry (bar chart)
- Layoffs by country (bar chart)
- Funding vs layoffs (scatter plot)
- Correlation analysis (heatmap)

### 🔹 Multivariate Analysis
- Year-wise layoffs trend (line chart)
- Industry-wise trend over time
- Pivot tables for deeper insights

---

## 📈 Key Insights
- The technology industry experienced the highest layoffs
- Layoffs have increased significantly in recent years
- A small number of companies contributed to very large layoffs
- Layoffs are concentrated in specific countries
- No strong correlation between funding and layoffs

## 🧠 Business Insights (Plain English)
- Layoffs are not evenly distributed across industries
- Tech companies are most affected due to restructuring
- Economic conditions play a major role in layoffs
- Even financially strong companies reduce workforce
- Workforce reduction is driven by both strategy and external factors

---

## 💡 Recommendations
- Improve workforce planning to avoid sudden layoffs
- Invest in employee reskilling and upskilling
- Diversify business strategies to reduce risk
- Focus on cost optimization before layoffs
- Governments should support affected industries and employees

---

## 🏁 Conclusion
This project provides a comprehensive analysis of global layoffs trends. It highlights how layoffs are influenced by industry dynamics, economic conditions, and company strategies. The insights from this project can help organizations and decision-makers make informed workforce planning decisions.
